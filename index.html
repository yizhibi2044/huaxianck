Artist Statement
Du Huaxian
杜华显

My work begins with looking—obsessively, accumulating. Over the past years I have photographed and filmed more than ten thousand moments along China's coastlines and fishing communities: the weight of a body bent over a crate, the geometry of a dock at dawn, the way industrial light cuts across weathered clothing.

I am not interested in documentation. Where other painters render these subjects with photographic fidelity, I find myself compelled by something else: the color hidden inside labor, the abstract rhythms of collective movement, the way human figures dissolve into and re-emerge from their environment.

Trained in oil painting and shaped by a long conversation with Matisse, Picasso, van Gogh, and Hockney, I returned to sustained painting practice in 2016 after a significant pause. That return felt less like resumption than transformation: I came back not to paint what I saw, but to paint what I felt seeing.

The fishermen and dock workers in my paintings are not symbols of hardship or nostalgia. They are bodies in motion, color events, proof that dignity and beauty coexist in the most ordinary labor.
